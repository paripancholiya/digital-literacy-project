# 'Stay Safe Online' Prevention Checklist

As a Student Digital Ambassador, I recommend these 8 actionable tips for all college students in India:

->UPI PIN Rule (Financial): Remember that a UPI PIN is only required to SEND money, never to receive it. If someone asks you to scan a QR code and enter your PIN to "receive" a payment, it is a scam.
->Verify Before Paying (Financial): Before sending any money via UPI, always verify the recipient's name displayed in the app to ensure it matches the person you intend to pay.
->Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Activate 2FA on your VIT email, LinkedIn, and Instagram accounts to add a second layer of security beyond just your password.
->Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Banking: Never log in to your bank account or enter card details while connected to free public Wi-Fi (like at cafes or railway stations). Use a secure mobile hotspot instead.
->Audit App Permissions: Regularly check your phone settings and revoke "Camera," "Microphone," or "Gallery" access for apps that don't strictly need them.
->Spot Phishing Links: Do not click on links in SMS or WhatsApp messages claiming you have won a "Lucky Draw" or that your "KYC has expired." These are common traps to steal credentials.
->Use Strong, Unique Passwords: Use a passphrase (a string of random words) rather than a simple word. Avoid using the same password for your college portal and your social media.
->Secure Your Webcam: Use a physical webcam cover or a piece of tape when your camera is not in use to prevent unauthorized remote access (camfecting).

# How to Report Cybercrime in India

If you or someone you know is a victim of a digital scam:
->Immediate Helpline: Call 1930 (National Cyber Crime Helpline).
->Official Portal: Register a complaint at cybercrime.gov.in.
