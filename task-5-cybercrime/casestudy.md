Case Study: The "QR Code" UPI Payment Fraud

Type of Crime: UPI/Online Payment Fraud (Social Engineering)

What the Crime Is:
UPI fraud involves tricking a user into authorizing a debit transaction from their bank account under the guise of receiving money. This is a form of social engineering where the attacker exploits the victim's lack of technical knowledge regarding how Unified Payments Interface (UPI) apps function.

Step-by-Step Scenario:

The Hook: A student lists an item for sale on an online marketplace (like OLX). An attacker contacts them immediately, posing as an interested buyer.

The Deception: The attacker claims they want to pay an advance to "book" the item and sends a QR code via WhatsApp.

The Trap: The attacker instructs the student to "Scan this code to receive the payment."

The Execution: When the student scans the code in their UPI app (like Google Pay or PhonePe), the app asks for their UPI PIN.

The Theft: In the excitement, the student enters their PIN. Instead of receiving money, the transaction deducts money from the student's account and sends it to the attacker.

Target Audience:
College students and young adults who frequently use digital wallets for peer-to-peer transactions are the primary targets, especially those unfamiliar with the rule that a PIN is only required to send money, never to receive it.

Consequences:
The immediate consequence is the loss of personal funds, which are often difficult to recover once the UPI transaction is settled. Furthermore, it leads to a loss of digital confidence and potential compromise of linked banking details if the attacker uses the interaction to deploy further phishing links.
